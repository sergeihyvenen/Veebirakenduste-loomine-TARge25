Briards

Furball dog

The briard, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog.

Breed history

The Berger de Brie was first shown at the first Paris dog show in 1863,
where a bitch named Charmant took a prize. The first Briard to be registered
in the Livre des Origines Françaises [fr], the national stud-book for dogs,
was Sans Gêne, winner of a gold medal at the Paris show in 1885.
A breed standard was drawn up in 1897, in which two varieties were
described: one with a woolly or sheep-like coat, the other with a coat more
similar to that of the goat; the woolly type later disappeared. In 1909 a breed
society, Les Amis du Briard, was formed.
